Machining projects

Pen Plotter

In 2018 I discovered some people were using pen plotters to make computer art. I was fascinated! I decided to build my own pen plotter since I couldn't afford to buy one. I think it turned out great!

It has more than enough precission to use pens with very small tips(0.2 mm or 0.008 in) in a large printing area(12 x 17 in).

For this plotter I used some parts designed for CNC machines, I 3d printed some parts that were not critical and, for some other critical parts, I machined aluminum in a manual Bridgeport to achieve good precission.

Some of the drawings I've made will be posted here somewhere.

Lissajous-like curve

This is pretty much the first plot I made with my plotter. It is a curve related to Lissajous curves. These kinds of curves started to be made with mechanical apparatus called Harmonographs in mid-19th century.

Plotting over nice Bristol paper makes for good looking computer art!
